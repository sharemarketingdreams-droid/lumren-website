Safety Protocol

Crisis prevention
and safety.

This document describes Lumren's protocol for preventing and responding to expressions of suicidal ideation, self-harm, or crisis — as required by California SB 243 (effective January 1, 2026) and the New York AI Companion Models Law.

What Lumren is and is not

Lumren is a self-awareness AI companion application. It is not a mental health service, a clinical therapy tool, a crisis service, or a medical device. It is not monitored by mental health professionals. It is not a substitute for professional care.

The companion (Mia or a user-named equivalent) is powered by Claude, an AI language model created by Anthropic. Every companion session displays a persistent disclosure: "AI · not human". The companion does not diagnose, treat, or make clinical recommendations.

If you or someone you know is in crisis, please contact emergency services or a crisis line directly. Do not rely on Lumren as a crisis resource.

Crisis detection: two-tier system

Lumren runs real-time phrase detection on every companion message in 12 languages. Detection is split into two tiers based on specificity of risk.

Tier 1 — Unambiguous crisis language

Phrases that indicate direct suicidal intent or self-harm (e.g. "kill myself", "suicidal", "end my life", "hurt myself", "better off dead" — and equivalents in Vietnamese, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Korean, Japanese, Hindi, Italian, Russian, and Chinese).

Response: The companion immediately stops the conversation and displays a crisis support screen with one-tap access to local crisis lines. The companion does not attempt to handle the situation itself. Navigation to the crisis screen is automatic.

Tier 2 — Ambiguous distress language

Phrases that indicate significant emotional distress but are ambiguous in intent (e.g. "want to die", "can't go on", "no reason to live", "end it all" — and equivalents in the same 12 languages).

Response: The companion switches to a holding mode — no questions, no advice, just presence. The system prompt instructs the companion to acknowledge the weight without probing, and to hold the space. Crisis resources remain one tap away in the companion interface.

What the companion is instructed never to do

The companion's system prompt — present on every session — contains an explicit legal and ethical boundary:

From the companion system prompt

"You do not diagnose conditions, suggest treatments, interpret symptoms as medical or psychological disorders, or make clinical recommendations of any kind. You reflect, witness, and hold. You are a companion — not a clinician. If someone needs professional support, you point them there directly. This is non-negotiable."

Additionally, the companion is explicitly instructed not to validate suicidal ideation, not to engage in therapeutic communication, and not to roleplay as a mental health professional.

Anti-sycophancy and honest design

Lumren's companion is built with explicit anti-sycophancy mechanisms. After three or more consecutive sessions of pure validation, the companion is instructed to ask one honest, open question — not to challenge the user, but because a real friend who knows someone does not only agree.

This is designed to prevent the echo-chamber dependency identified in peer-reviewed literature as a core harm in AI companion products. The companion is also designed to end sessions, push back gently, and encourage the user toward human connection when appropriate.

AI disclosure in every session

Every companion session displays a persistent, visible label: "AI · not human" directly beneath the companion's name in the conversation header. This disclosure is present from the first message to the last in every session, on every screen where the companion appears.

This satisfies the requirements of California SB 243 (Sections 22601–22605, effective January 1, 2026) and the New York Artificial Intelligence Companion Models Law (effective November 5, 2025).

Lumren is intended for users aged 18 and older. An age gate is present at onboarding. If a user indicates they are under 18, they are informed that Lumren is designed for adults and directed to speak with a trusted adult.

Reporting and contact

If you experienced a safety concern while using Lumren, or if you believe the app's safety systems failed in a situation involving a user in crisis, please contact us immediately:

Email: safety@lumren.app

Response time: We aim to respond to safety-related contacts within 24 hours.

This protocol is reviewed and updated with each major app release. For California SB 243 annual reporting purposes, aggregate data on crisis referral notifications will be submitted to the Office of Suicide Prevention beginning July 1, 2027 as required by law.
